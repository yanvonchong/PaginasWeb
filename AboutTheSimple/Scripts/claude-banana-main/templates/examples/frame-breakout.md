# Frame Breakout

## Domain Mode
Dynamic Illustration

## Technique
Frame Breakout

## Prompt Template

A dramatic composition where {character} is physically breaking through {frame_type}, shattering the boundary between the contained image and the surrounding white void. The character is captured mid-{action}, with parts of their body — an outstretched hand, a bent knee, flowing hair — extending beyond the frame's edge into the empty space outside. {effects} burst outward from the point of breakout, scattering fragments of the frame itself as debris. Inside the frame, the scene retains its original color palette and internal logic, while everything outside the frame exists on a clean white background, creating a powerful contrast between the two spatial planes. The perspective is slightly low-angle, giving the character a sense of power and forward momentum. Cracks radiate outward from the breakout point along the frame's border, and the frame itself bends and warps under the force of the character's motion. The overall feeling is one of energy, liberation, and explosive movement frozen in a single instant.

## Variables

- `{character}`: The subject breaking free — e.g., "a female warrior in silver plate armor with a glowing longsword," "a street dancer in mid-spin wearing a neon tracksuit"
- `{frame_type}`: The 2D boundary being shattered — e.g., "a gilded baroque picture frame," "a comic book panel with thick black borders," "a smartphone screen"
- `{action}`: The motion being performed — e.g., "leap, lunging forward with sword extended," "kick, one foot already outside the frame"
- `{effects}`: Visual energy emanating from the break — e.g., "shards of golden frame wood and sparkling light particles," "ink splatter and halftone dots from the shattered panel"

## Aspect Ratio
3:4

## Usage Notes
Frame breakout images are ideal for posters, social media graphics, and any context where you want to convey energy and boundary-defying dynamism. This technique works well with Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview), which handles the spatial contradiction between framed and unframed space effectively. Keep the background outside the frame simple (white or minimal gradient) so the breakout effect reads clearly.
