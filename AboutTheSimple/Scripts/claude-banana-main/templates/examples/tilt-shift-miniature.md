# Tilt-Shift Miniature

## Domain Mode
Photography Simulation

## Technique
Tilt-Shift Miniature

## Prompt Template

An overhead tilt-shift photograph looking down at a miniature-scale {scene_theme}, where the selective focus and saturated colors create the convincing illusion that an entire real-world location is actually a tiny handcrafted model. The scene features {key_buildings} arranged in a dense, charming layout with narrow streets and pathways winding between them. Scattered throughout are {tiny_figures} going about their routines, each no larger than a grain of rice from this vantage point. The color palette leans into {color_palette}, with the saturation boosted slightly beyond natural levels to enhance the toy-like appearance. A strong depth-of-field blur softens the top and bottom thirds of the frame, leaving only a narrow band of razor-sharp focus across the middle, which is the hallmark of tilt-shift optics. The overhead angle is roughly forty-five degrees, providing enough perspective to see building facades while emphasizing rooftops and ground-level activity. Tiny vehicles, street lamps, and trees with cotton-like canopies complete the diorama illusion.

## Variables

- `{scene_theme}`: The type of location or setting — e.g., "a European Christmas market," "a Japanese fishing village," "a 1950s American main street"
- `{key_buildings}`: Notable architectural features — e.g., "half-timbered market stalls with red and white striped awnings, a stone clock tower, and a small cathedral," "wooden boat sheds, a lighthouse, and terraced rice paddies on the hillside"
- `{tiny_figures}`: Human activity at miniature scale — e.g., "shoppers carrying bags, a vendor roasting chestnuts, children gathered around a carousel," "fishermen hauling nets, a woman hanging laundry, a cat sleeping on a wall"
- `{color_palette}`: Dominant color scheme — e.g., "warm reds, golds, and evergreen," "ocean teal, weathered wood brown, and white foam"

## Aspect Ratio
16:9

## Usage Notes
Tilt-shift miniature images are immediately eye-catching due to their uncanny diorama effect. Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) handles the selective focus simulation and saturated color boost convincingly. For maximum impact, choose scenes with lots of small repeated details — markets, harbors, train stations — as the density reinforces the model-world illusion.

## Example Output

An overhead tilt-shift photograph looking down at a miniature-scale European Christmas market, where the selective focus and saturated colors create the convincing illusion that an entire real-world cobblestone square is actually a tiny handcrafted model. The scene features half-timbered market stalls with red and white striped awnings, a stone clock tower with a glowing face, and a small Gothic cathedral with snow dusting its spires, arranged in a dense, charming layout with narrow cobblestone pathways winding between them. Scattered throughout are shoppers carrying bags, a vendor roasting chestnuts over a tiny brazier, and children gathered around a spinning carousel with golden horses, each no larger than a grain of rice from this vantage point. The color palette leans into warm reds, golds, and evergreen, with the saturation boosted slightly beyond natural levels to enhance the toy-like appearance. A strong depth-of-field blur softens the top and bottom thirds of the frame, leaving only a narrow band of razor-sharp focus across the middle, which is the hallmark of tilt-shift optics. The overhead angle is roughly forty-five degrees, providing enough perspective to see building facades while emphasizing snow-covered rooftops and ground-level activity. Tiny delivery trucks, wrought-iron street lamps wound with garland, and trees with cotton-like snow-laden canopies complete the diorama illusion.
