# Product Showcase

## Domain Mode
Product

## Technique
Three-point studio lighting with hero placement

## Prompt Template

A commercial product photograph of {product}, crafted from {material}, presented as the singular hero object on a clean studio set. The lighting rig uses a three-point configuration: a large softbox key light at forty-five degrees camera right producing gentle specular highlights that reveal the material's texture, a fill card on the opposite side lifting shadow detail without eliminating depth, and a rim light from behind that separates the product from {background}. The brand context evokes {brand_context}, informing the color temperature, prop styling, and overall visual language. The product is shot at a slight low angle to lend it stature and presence, with a 90mm macro lens maintaining edge-to-edge sharpness. Reflections on the surface beneath the product are controlled and subtle, adding dimension without distraction. The final image is color-graded for commercial print, with neutral whites and accurate material rendering throughout.

## Variables

- `{product}`: The item being photographed — e.g., "a matte black wireless earbud case," "a hand-thrown ceramic pour-over dripper," "a titanium mechanical watch"
- `{material}`: Primary material and finish — e.g., "brushed aluminum with satin coating," "hand-stitched full-grain leather," "borosilicate glass with copper accents"
- `{brand_context}`: The aspirational brand world — e.g., "Scandinavian minimalism akin to Bang & Olufsen," "Japanese wabi-sabi craft heritage," "Italian luxury automotive detailing"
- `{background}`: Backdrop treatment — e.g., "a seamless matte charcoal sweep," "a slab of raw Carrara marble," "a gradient from warm ivory to soft grey"

## Aspect Ratio
4:5

## Usage Notes
Purpose-built for e-commerce hero images, pitch decks, and social media product launches. The three-point lighting language is particularly effective with Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) when paired with specific lens and material descriptors. Avoid adding lifestyle context — this template is about the product in isolation.
