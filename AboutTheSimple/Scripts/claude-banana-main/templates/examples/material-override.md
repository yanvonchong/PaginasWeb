# Material Override

## Domain Mode
Concept Art

## Technique
Material Transformation

## Prompt Template

A striking studio photograph of {object}, except the entire object has been meticulously reconstructed out of {material}. Every surface, seam, and contour faithfully preserves the original form and proportions, but the material replacement is total and convincing — the viewer can see the texture, grain, weight, and reflective properties of the new material in every detail. The object sits on a clean surface under {lighting}, which reveals the material's unique characteristics: its translucency or opacity, the way it catches highlights, and the micro-texture visible at close range. The {background} provides a simple but complementary backdrop that keeps the focus entirely on the transformed object. Small contextual clues reinforce the material — a faint condensation ring if ice, hairline fractures if ceramic, a subtle patina if metal. The photograph is composed tightly, shot with a macro-influenced lens that captures surface detail while maintaining the full object in frame. The overall effect is both familiar and deeply strange, turning an everyday item into a museum-worthy sculptural curiosity.

## Variables

- `{object}`: The everyday item being transformed — e.g., "a pair of running sneakers with laces tied," "a rotary telephone," "a vintage typewriter"
- `{material}`: The unexpected replacement material — e.g., "hand-blown Murano glass with swirling cobalt veins," "rough-hewn granite," "living moss and tiny ferns," "melting chocolate"
- `{lighting}`: Studio light setup — e.g., "a single dramatic side light with warm color temperature," "cool diffused overhead softbox lighting," "backlit rim light creating a glowing silhouette edge"
- `{background}`: Backdrop description — e.g., "a seamless matte black backdrop," "a raw concrete surface with a pale gray wall," "a gradient from dark navy to white"

## Aspect Ratio
1:1

## Usage Notes
Material transformation images create instant visual intrigue by pairing a recognizable form with an impossible substance. Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) excels at material rendering, producing convincing texture and reflective properties. Choose materials with strong visual identity — glass, stone, vegetation, food — and objects with distinctive silhouettes for the clearest read.

## Example Output

A striking studio photograph of a pair of running sneakers with laces tied in neat bows, except the entire pair has been meticulously reconstructed out of hand-blown Murano glass with swirling cobalt and gold veins running through the translucent body. Every surface, seam, and contour faithfully preserves the original sneaker form and proportions — the stitching lines, the sole tread pattern, the tongue and eyelets — but the material replacement is total and convincing, with the viewer able to see the glossy, slightly bubbly texture and deep luminous color of the glass in every detail. The sneakers sit on a polished black marble surface under a single dramatic side light with warm color temperature, which reveals the glass's unique characteristics: light passing through the thinner panels of the upper, bright specular highlights on the toe cap, and the swirling veins glowing where the light penetrates deepest. A seamless matte black backdrop provides a simple but complementary setting that keeps the focus entirely on the transformed sneakers. Small contextual clues reinforce the material — faint internal air bubbles frozen in the glass, and the way the sole meets the surface with a hard crystalline edge rather than rubber flex. The photograph is composed tightly, shot with a macro-influenced lens that captures surface detail while maintaining both sneakers in frame. The overall effect is both familiar and deeply strange, turning everyday athletic shoes into a museum-worthy sculptural curiosity.
