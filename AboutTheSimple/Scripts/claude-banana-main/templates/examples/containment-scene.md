# Containment Scene

## Domain Mode
Fantasy Illustration

## Technique
Containment Scenes

## Prompt Template

A breathtaking miniature world contained inside {container}, viewed at eye level against a softly blurred background. Within the transparent walls, an entire {world_theme} ecosystem thrives in impossibly fine detail. The interior features {key_elements}, all scaled down to fit perfectly within the vessel while maintaining a sense of depth and layered dimensionality. Tiny atmospheric effects like mist, floating particles, or gentle light rays move through the enclosed space, giving the scene a living, breathing quality. The {lighting} illuminates the interior, casting delicate caustic patterns through the transparent surface onto the ground beneath the container. The exterior surface shows subtle reflections and refractions that warp the miniature world slightly at the curved edges. A few elements — a tiny vine, a wisp of smoke, a stray butterfly — appear to escape from the opening or seams of the container, bridging the boundary between the micro-world and the outer reality.

## Variables

- `{container}`: The transparent enclosure — e.g., "a crystal potion bottle with a cork stopper," "a vintage light bulb," "a cracked hourglass"
- `{world_theme}`: The biome or setting inside — e.g., "enchanted autumn forest," "deep-sea bioluminescent cave," "floating sky islands"
- `{key_elements}`: Specific details populating the world — e.g., "a mossy waterfall cascading into a glowing pool, tiny mushroom houses, and fireflies," "coral towers, a sunken pirate ship, and schools of neon fish"
- `{lighting}`: Light source and quality — e.g., "warm golden hour sunlight streaming from the upper left," "cool bioluminescent glow radiating from within"

## Aspect Ratio
2:3

## Usage Notes
Containment scenes create a sense of wonder by juxtaposing vast landscapes within tiny vessels. Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) handles the scale contradiction and transparent material refractions well. For best results, choose containers with interesting shapes and world themes with strong vertical layering so the miniature feels genuinely deep.

## Example Output

A breathtaking miniature world contained inside a crystal potion bottle with a cork stopper and a thin hemp cord wrapped around its neck, viewed at eye level against a softly blurred background. Within the transparent walls, an entire enchanted autumn forest ecosystem thrives in impossibly fine detail. The interior features a mossy waterfall cascading into a glowing turquoise pool, tiny mushroom houses with warm window light, a winding stone path, and clusters of fireflies drifting between orange and crimson maple trees, all scaled down to fit perfectly within the vessel while maintaining a sense of depth and layered dimensionality. Tiny atmospheric effects like golden mist, floating spores, and gentle light rays move through the enclosed space, giving the scene a living, breathing quality. Warm golden hour sunlight streaming from the upper left illuminates the interior, casting delicate caustic patterns through the transparent glass onto the wooden table beneath the bottle. The exterior surface shows subtle reflections and refractions that warp the miniature forest slightly at the curved edges. A few elements — a tiny vine creeping over the cork, a wisp of golden smoke, and a stray monarch butterfly — appear to escape from the opening of the container, bridging the boundary between the micro-world and the outer reality.
