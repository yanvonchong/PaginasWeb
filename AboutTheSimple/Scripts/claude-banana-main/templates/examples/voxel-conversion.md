# Voxel Conversion

## Domain Mode
3D Icon Design

## Technique
Voxel Conversion

## Prompt Template

A clean 3D voxel art rendering of {subject}, built entirely from small cubic blocks arranged on an invisible grid, reminiscent of a polished Minecraft aesthetic or a modern voxel game asset. The color scheme uses {color_scheme}, applied in flat, unshaded tones to each block face with subtle ambient occlusion darkening the crevices between cubes to give depth and readability. The object is viewed from a {angle} perspective, presenting a clear and iconic silhouette that makes the subject instantly recognizable despite the blocky abstraction. The {background} contrasts cleanly with the voxel subject, ensuring the form pops visually. Each voxel is crisply defined with sharp edges and no anti-aliasing blur, maintaining the pixelated charm of the medium. The overall scale is compact and icon-friendly — the subject is reduced to its most essential geometric forms, with just enough block detail to capture defining features without overcomplicating the shape. The lighting is soft and even, with a faint directional highlight on the upper-left faces of each cube to suggest dimensionality.

## Variables

- `{subject}`: The object or creature being voxelized — e.g., "a red fox sitting with its tail curled around its paws," "a vintage camera with a leather strap," "a medieval castle with towers and a drawbridge"
- `{color_scheme}`: Color palette for the voxels — e.g., "warm oranges, creams, and charcoal," "pastel rainbow gradient," "monochrome gray with a single accent of electric blue"
- `{angle}`: Camera viewpoint — e.g., "three-quarter isometric," "straight-on front view," "slightly elevated top-down"
- `{background}`: Backdrop treatment — e.g., "a clean white void," "a soft gradient from sky blue to white," "a dark charcoal surface with subtle grid lines"

## Aspect Ratio
1:1

## Usage Notes
Voxel conversions produce charming, instantly readable icons and game-style assets. Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) maintains cubic grid consistency and clean isometric projection well. For best results, choose subjects with strong, recognizable silhouettes and limit the color palette to five or fewer hues to keep the voxel look cohesive and readable at small sizes.

## Example Output

A clean 3D voxel art rendering of a red fox sitting with its tail curled gracefully around its front paws, built entirely from small cubic blocks arranged on an invisible grid, reminiscent of a polished Minecraft aesthetic or a modern voxel game asset. The color scheme uses warm oranges, creams, and charcoal, applied in flat, unshaded tones to each block face — rich burnt orange for the fur, cream for the chest and tail tip, and charcoal for the paws, nose, and ear tips — with subtle ambient occlusion darkening the crevices between cubes to give depth and readability. The fox is viewed from a three-quarter isometric perspective, presenting a clear and iconic silhouette that makes the animal instantly recognizable despite the blocky abstraction. A clean white void contrasts cleanly with the warm-toned voxel fox, ensuring the form pops visually. Each voxel is crisply defined with sharp edges and no anti-aliasing blur, maintaining the pixelated charm of the medium. The overall scale is compact and icon-friendly — the fox is reduced to its most essential geometric forms, with just enough block detail to capture the pointed ears, narrow snout, and bushy tail without overcomplicating the shape. The lighting is soft and even, with a faint directional highlight on the upper-left faces of each cube to suggest dimensionality.
