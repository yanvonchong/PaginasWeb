# Product Flat Lay

## Domain Mode
Product

## Technique
Overhead knolling composition with controlled negative space

## Prompt Template

A top-down flat lay photograph of {items} arranged in a precise knolling composition on {surface}. Every object is aligned to an invisible grid, rotated to parallel or perpendicular orientations, with deliberate negative space between each element creating visual breathing room. The color palette is restricted to {color_palette}, unifying the disparate objects into a cohesive graphic statement. The thematic concept driving the arrangement is {theme}, which informs both the selection of props and the spatial hierarchy — the most important item occupies the optical center while supporting pieces radiate outward in decreasing scale. Lighting is flat and even, produced by a large overhead diffusion panel that eliminates harsh shadows while preserving enough micro-shadow to define edges and material differences. The camera is mounted on a copy stand, perfectly perpendicular to the surface, shot at f/11 for maximum depth of field. The result reads as equal parts editorial styling and information design.

## Variables

- `{items}`: The collection of objects — e.g., "a leather journal, fountain pen, brass compass, linen handkerchief, and wax seal kit," "espresso cups, coffee beans, a hand grinder, and a Chemex," "art supplies including gouache tubes, brushes, a palette knife, and mixing dishes"
- `{surface}`: The base material — e.g., "a weathered white oak tabletop," "a sheet of kraft paper," "polished black slate," "linen fabric with visible weave"
- `{color_palette}`: Dominant tones — e.g., "warm neutrals: cream, tan, cognac, and matte gold," "monochrome greyscale with a single pop of vermillion," "earth tones: terracotta, olive, and raw sienna"
- `{theme}`: The conceptual thread — e.g., "the gentleman traveler's desk," "morning ritual," "the analog creative's toolkit," "weekend camping essentials"

## Aspect Ratio
1:1

## Usage Notes
Flat lays are a staple of Instagram, editorial features, and brand storytelling. This template enforces the knolling discipline (everything at right angles) which produces cleaner, more professional results than casual scatter arrangements. Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) handles the overhead perspective and material differentiation well. For best results, keep item count between five and nine objects to avoid visual clutter.
