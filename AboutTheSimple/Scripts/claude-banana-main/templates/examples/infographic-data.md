# Infographic Data

## Domain Mode
Infographic

## Technique
Modular grid system with isometric data visualization

## Prompt Template

A clean, information-dense infographic on the topic of {topic}, presenting {data_points} as its core content. The visual style follows a {style_direction} design language, with all elements organized on a modular grid that allows the eye to navigate naturally from the headline through supporting data to the conclusion. The color scheme is {color_scheme}, applied hierarchically: the primary color anchors headlines and key statistics, the secondary color supports charts and callout boxes, and neutral tones handle body text and background panels. Data is visualized through a mix of chart types — bar graphs, circular progress indicators, icon arrays, and annotated diagrams — each chosen for clarity rather than novelty. Typographic hierarchy is strict: a bold display typeface for the main headline, a medium-weight sans-serif for section headers, and a light-weight reading face for body copy, all maintaining consistent vertical rhythm. Icons are rendered in a unified line-weight style, simple enough to read at small sizes. The overall layout flows vertically with clear section breaks, and a subtle background texture or pattern adds visual interest without competing with the data. Every number is presented with context — comparisons, percentages, or scale indicators — so that the information communicates at a glance.

## Variables

- `{topic}`: The subject matter — e.g., "the global water crisis," "the lifecycle of a coffee bean from farm to cup," "how remote work has changed urban migration patterns," "the anatomy of a lithium-ion battery"
- `{data_points}`: The specific information to visualize — e.g., "five key statistics about water scarcity, consumption by sector, and projected shortages by 2050," "the seven stages of coffee production with yield percentages at each step," "three comparative datasets showing population shifts in major cities from 2019 to 2025"
- `{style_direction}`: The design personality — e.g., "editorial and sophisticated, inspired by The Economist's data pages," "playful and illustrated, with hand-drawn icon accents," "corporate and precise, suitable for a boardroom presentation," "minimalist and Swiss-inspired with strict grid adherence"
- `{color_scheme}`: The palette — e.g., "navy blue primary, coral accent, light grey background," "forest green and gold on white," "monochromatic slate blue with a single highlight of amber," "rich burgundy and cream with charcoal text"

## Aspect Ratio
9:16

## Usage Notes
AI-generated infographics are strongest as layout and style references — the specific numbers and text will rarely be accurate and should be treated as placeholder content for actual data. This template excels at establishing visual direction for designers. Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) produces convincing infographic layouts with clean typography hierarchy. For best results, keep the data points description specific and limited to three to five key metrics to avoid visual overload.
