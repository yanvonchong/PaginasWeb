# Abstract Generative

## Domain Mode
Abstract

## Technique
Mathematical pattern generation with organic variation

## Prompt Template

An abstract generative artwork built from {pattern_type} as its foundational structure, rendered in {medium}. The color palette is constrained to {color_palette}, applied with intentional variation in saturation and value to create depth within the flat picture plane. The composition conveys a sense of {movement}, achieved through the progressive transformation of the base pattern — elements gradually shifting in scale, rotation, density, or opacity as they move across the canvas. The mathematical precision of the underlying grid is softened by controlled imperfections: slight jitter in positioning, organic variation in line weight, and occasional breaks in the pattern that feel deliberate rather than erroneous. The interplay between order and entropy is the conceptual core of the piece. At a distance, the work reads as a unified field of color and rhythm; at close inspection, individual elements reveal their geometric construction and the rules governing their behavior. The overall tone is contemplative rather than aggressive, favoring subtlety and slow revelation over immediate spectacle. Negative space is treated as an active compositional element, not merely the absence of marks.

## Variables

- `{pattern_type}`: The mathematical foundation — e.g., "Voronoi tessellation," "concentric Fibonacci spirals," "Penrose tiling with aperiodic symmetry," "flow field lines derived from Perlin noise," "reaction-diffusion Turing patterns"
- `{color_palette}`: The restricted color set — e.g., "midnight blue, cerulean, and titanium white," "analogous warm tones from cadmium yellow through burnt orange to deep crimson," "monochromatic grey with a single accent of gold leaf," "pastel gradients from lavender to seafoam"
- `{movement}`: The kinetic quality — e.g., "slow centrifugal expansion from the center," "lateral drift like wind across a field," "vertical ascension with increasing turbulence," "rhythmic pulsation between compression and release"
- `{medium}`: The rendering style — e.g., "vector graphics with clean edges and flat fills," "watercolor washes bleeding into wet paper," "risograph print with halftone dots and slight misregistration," "pen plotter drawing with archival ink on cotton paper"

## Aspect Ratio
1:1

## Usage Notes
Generative and abstract art prompts benefit enormously from naming specific mathematical patterns and artistic media, which give the model concrete visual references rather than vague aesthetic descriptors. This template works well with Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) for both vector-like outputs and organic media simulations. The key to a successful result is the tension between the mathematical pattern and its organic disruption.
