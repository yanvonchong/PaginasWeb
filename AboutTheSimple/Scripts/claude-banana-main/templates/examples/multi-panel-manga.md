# Multi-Panel Manga

## Domain Mode
Sequential Art

## Technique
Sequential Panel Storytelling

## Prompt Template

A {panel_count}-panel manga page in black and white with screentone shading, depicting {character} experiencing an emotional arc that moves through {emotion_arc}. The panels are arranged in a dynamic manga layout with varied sizes — key emotional beats receive larger panels while transitional moments are compressed into smaller ones. The setting is {setting}, rendered with detailed background work in the establishing panel and increasingly abstracted backgrounds as emotions intensify, following the manga convention of using speed lines, tone bursts, and white voids to externalize inner feeling. Each panel advances the emotional progression clearly through facial expressions, body language, and compositional choices — close-ups for intimacy, wide shots for isolation, dutch angles for tension. Speech bubbles and thought bubbles are present but empty, left as placeholder shapes for future text. The line work is confident with varied thickness, bold outlines on figures and finer detail lines on environments. The page reads right-to-left in traditional manga fashion, with clear gutters separating each panel.

## Variables

- `{character}`: The protagonist of the sequence — e.g., "a teenage boy in a school uniform with messy hair and a scar on his cheek," "a middle-aged woman in a business suit clutching a crumpled letter"
- `{panel_count}`: Number of panels on the page — e.g., "four," "six," "five"
- `{emotion_arc}`: The progression of feelings across panels — e.g., "curiosity to shock to quiet determination," "joy to confusion to heartbreak to fragile hope," "calm focus to explosive rage to exhausted regret"
- `{setting}`: Environment where the scene takes place — e.g., "a rain-soaked rooftop at night with city lights below," "a sunlit classroom with dust motes in the air," "a crowded train platform during rush hour"

## Aspect Ratio
2:3

## Usage Notes
This template generates manga-style sequential art pages with genuine emotional storytelling. Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) handles the black-and-white linework and screentone shading characteristic of manga effectively. For clearest results, limit the panel count to four to six panels and keep the emotion arc to three or four distinct beats — too many shifts become muddled in a single image generation.

## Example Output

A six-panel manga page in black and white with screentone shading, depicting a teenage boy in a wrinkled school uniform with messy dark hair and a scar on his left cheek experiencing an emotional arc that moves through calm focus to surprise to disbelief to explosive anger to hollow silence to fragile resolve. The panels are arranged in a dynamic manga layout with varied sizes — the anger panel dominates the upper right as the largest frame, while the transitional surprise and disbelief moments are compressed into smaller horizontal strips. The setting is a rain-soaked rooftop at night with city lights blurred below, rendered with detailed background work in the establishing panel showing the full rooftop with puddles and a chain-link fence, and increasingly abstracted backgrounds as emotions intensify — the anger panel replacing the cityscape entirely with radiating speed lines and shattered screentone. Each panel advances the emotional progression clearly: a calm medium shot of the boy standing at the railing, a close-up of widening eyes as he reads a phone screen, a tilted panel of his trembling hand, a dynamic full-body shot of him slamming his fist into the fence with impact lines radiating outward, a stark panel of him slumped on the wet ground in a pool of white negative space, and a final close-up of his eyes — wet but steady — looking upward. Speech bubbles and thought bubbles are present but empty, left as placeholder shapes for future text. The line work is confident with varied thickness, bold outlines on the boy and finer detail lines on the rain and city lights. The page reads right-to-left in traditional manga fashion, with clear gutters separating each panel.
