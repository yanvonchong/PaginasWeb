# Landscape Atmospheric

## Domain Mode
Landscape

## Technique
Multi-layer atmospheric perspective with foreground anchoring

## Prompt Template

A richly layered landscape depicting a {biome} environment under {weather} conditions during {time_of_day}. The composition is structured in four distinct depth planes. In the immediate foreground, {foreground_element} provides scale and tactile detail, rendered with crisp focus and visible texture. The near-middle ground introduces the primary terrain features — landforms, vegetation patterns, or water bodies — that define the biome's character. The far-middle ground recedes into atmospheric perspective, colors desaturating and contrast flattening as distance increases, creating a natural sense of depth without artificial manipulation. The distant background dissolves into sky and weather, where the horizon line is placed at the lower third to emphasize the scale of the atmosphere above. Volumetric light interacts with the weather conditions, producing god rays, diffused glow, or color-shifted illumination depending on the atmospheric density. The palette is driven entirely by the natural interaction of light and environment — no artificial color grading. Every element serves the sense of immersion: wind direction is consistent across vegetation and cloud movement, water reflects the actual sky color, and shadows fall at angles consistent with the light source position.

## Variables

- `{biome}`: The ecosystem type — e.g., "temperate old-growth rainforest," "high-altitude alpine meadow," "coastal volcanic shoreline," "boreal taiga in autumn"
- `{weather}`: Atmospheric conditions — e.g., "a storm cell breaking apart to reveal patches of blue," "dense valley fog burning off," "steady drizzle with low visibility," "crystalline cold with ice fog"
- `{time_of_day}`: The lighting moment — e.g., "the first ten minutes after sunrise," "high noon with the sun directly overhead," "civil twilight with Venus visible on the horizon," "the violet hour between sunset and darkness"
- `{foreground_element}`: The anchoring detail — e.g., "a moss-covered granite boulder with ferns growing from its cracks," "a weathered wooden fence post with a coil of rusted wire," "a tide pool reflecting the sky among volcanic rock"

## Aspect Ratio
16:9

## Usage Notes
This template is designed for landscapes that feel inhabitable — places you could walk into. The four-layer depth structure is the key differentiator from simpler landscape prompts, and the foreground element is essential for grounding the viewer. Nano Banana Pro (gemini-3.1-flash-image-preview) renders atmospheric perspective and volumetric light exceptionally well. For maximum impact, choose weather and time combinations that create dramatic light — storms at golden hour, fog at dawn, clear skies at twilight.
